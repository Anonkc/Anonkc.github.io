Scenario Overview

A suspicious USB drive was found in the parking lot of Rhetorical Hospital. As a member of the cybersecurity team, the USB was analyzed using a virtual machine to prevent possible infection of live systems.

Attacker Mindset

The attacker could use timesheets or employee records to gather intelligence about staff routines. This information could enable targeted phishing or impersonation attacks. For example, an attacker could craft emails that appear to come from a known coworker, increasing the chance of a successful social engineering attempt.

Risk Analysis

  • Managerial Control: Promote employee training on USB safety and reporting protocols.
  • Operational Control: Enforce regular antivirus scans across all devices, especially lab/testing environments.
  • Technical Control: Disable AutoPlay on corporate machines to prevent auto-execution of files.
  • Policy Enforcement: Restrict USB testing to trained IT staff in sandboxed environments.
